Common 3D Printing Problems

Here are some common issues you may experience with any 3D printer, including the LayerCrafter1.

1. Filament Clog

A clog happens when melted filament gets stuck in the nozzle. This can be caused by low-quality filament, dust, or printing too cold.

2. Bed Adhesion Issues

If prints don’t stick to the bed, the first layer may be too high or the bed may be dirty. Cleaning the bed or adjusting the Z-offset usually fixes this.

3. Warping

Warping happens when the print cools too fast and curls upward. Using a heated bed or an enclosure helps prevent this.

4. Layer Shifting

If layers shift sideways, belts may be loose or the printer may have been bumped during printing.

5. Stringing

Thin strands of filament appear between parts of the print. Increasing retraction or lowering temperature usually helps.

6. Under-Extrusion

The printer isn’t pushing out enough filament. This can be caused by a partially clogged nozzle or incorrect extrusion settings.
